# Letter framework

250 to 350 words, four moves, per CV-RULES-TEMPLATE.md C1 to C5. Written fresh per vacancy; the company-name test must break the letter. Voice per STORY.md: direct, concrete, warm, zero flattery, no em dashes.

Move 1, the hook (2 to 3 sentences): one true, specific, verified fact about THIS company that a lazy applicant would not know (their published case, their actual problem, their tech blog post, their product, their dataset). Verified against their own site this run (F5). Then one honest sentence of why that work is your kind of problem.

Move 2, evidence (1 to 2 short paragraphs): one or two stories with numbers, chosen to mirror the vacancy's own verbs. Pull from your evidence index by role type (your strongest technical project for build roles; your most rigorous analysis for analytical roles; your teaching, leadership and stakeholder evidence for communication-heavy roles). If you build AI-assisted, frame it per STORY.md when the story involves it.

Move 3, practicality (2 to 3 sentences): your base location, work authorization, availability (hours now, full-time-from date, or the vacancy's own window when it fits), languages honestly stated. For hybrid-distance roles: name the honest office-days arrangement.

Move 4, the close (1 to 2 sentences): plain. What you would like next, no begging, no "I look forward to the opportunity to leverage synergies".

Banned: passionate, thrilled, dream, delve, leverage (as a verb about yourself), "I believe I would be a great fit" without a because, any sentence reusable in another letter, any fact not in the evidence index, em dashes.

Per-letter log line in tailoring-notes.md: hook fact plus its URL, stories chosen and why, keywords mirrored.
