# CV rules, the operative rubric (TEMPLATE)

Every CV and letter this pipeline produces must pass every numbered rule. The honesty auditor and recruiter lens check against this list literally. Grounded in ATS-PRINCIPLES.md (verified claims) and STORY.md (positioning). The ATS format rules (section A) and screening reality (section D, E) are generic; personalize the contact block and any content rules marked `<...>` in SETUP.md. Updated only via the self-improvement protocol.

## A. Document format (hard rules)

A1. One single column, top to bottom. No tables, text boxes, sidebars, columns, icons, skill bars, graphics, or photos. Every fact is selectable text.
A2. Contact block at the very top of the body (never the page header/footer zone): name, <your city and country>, phone, <your email>, <your linkedin url>, <your github or portfolio url>, and a work-authorization line ("<nationality> (EU citizen, no work permit required)" or equivalent) when it is true and helps.
A3. Standard headings only: Profile, Core Skills, Projects, Experience, Education, Skills and Languages, Certificates. English throughout (or the vacancy's language).
A4. One A4 page. Two only when a vacancy explicitly rewards depth (rare at junior level; log the reason in tailoring-notes.md).
A5. Ship both cv.docx and a text-based cv.pdf. Upload DOCX when a portal asks for Word or when the ATS is known legacy (Taleo). Never an image or flattened PDF.
A6. File names: "<Your Name> - CV - <Company>.docx/pdf". No dates, no versions in the name.
A7. No em dashes anywhere. Plain professional fonts, 10.5 to 11.5 pt body.
A8. No birthdate, no photo, no full street address (city is enough), no marital status (unless a specific market clearly rewards otherwise).

## B. Content and tailoring (hard rules)

B1. Title line under the name mirrors the vacancy's own role title, honestly. Never a title you have not held; a candidate for a degree writes the honest graduate/student form.
B2. Profile: 2 to 3 lines, containing the vacancy's 3 strongest honest keyword matches and one concrete proof number. Rewritten per vacancy, never recycled.
B3. Core Skills block: 4 to 6 items, each honestly held, phrased exactly as the vacancy phrases them where true. Spell out clean acronyms both ways once: "machine learning (ML)". Spell out noisy two-letter tokens in full (for example always "Operations Research", never bare "OR" as a keyword).
B4. Projects section: 2 to 3 of your headline projects, chosen by relevance to THIS vacancy, each as 1 to 2 bullets: action verb, method and tool, verified number (from EVIDENCE-INDEX.md only). Repo or live-demo URL on the most relevant one.
B5. Experience: each real role with its evidence-index backing. Sensitive employers or numbers stay off per your own RULES.md; keep junior lines to one line each.
B6. Education: degrees with dates and (where strong and true) honors, GPA, or named top grades relevant to the vacancy. Never volunteer sensitive grades.
B7. Every number, grade, title, and date traces to profile\EVIDENCE-INDEX.md. A claim not in the index does not ship. The never-claim list is absolute.
B8. Keywords live inside achievement bullets, never as loose lists beyond the Core Skills block, never stuffed, never hidden.
B9. Availability stated where relevant (vacancy asks or internship): hours per week and start window per PROFILE.md terms.
B10. Section order flexes by target: project-led (Projects above Experience) for build-flavored roles; education-led (Education high) for consultancy, traineeships, and research institutes. Log the choice in tailoring-notes.md.

## C. Letter rules (hard rules)

C1. 250 to 350 words, four moves: (1) a hook naming something true and specific about this company that a lazy applicant would not know; (2) one or two evidence stories with numbers, matched to the vacancy's actual verbs; (3) practicality: base location, work authorization, hours, start date, languages honestly stated; (4) a plain close.
C2. Zero sentences reusable in another letter. The company name test: if swapping the company name leaves a sensible letter, it fails.
C3. No flattery, no "passionate", no "I have always dreamed", no AI-slop constructions, no em dashes. Voice per STORY.md: direct, concrete, warm.
C4. Every letter includes one honest line of why THIS domain fits you, grounded in a real project or experience of yours.
C5. Letters ship as letter.docx and letter.pdf, same naming pattern as the CV.

## D. Screening reality checks (soft rules, applied in ranking and packaging)

D1. Knockout answers must be answerable cleanly before packaging: work authorization, location (per your commute rule), availability (per PROFILE.md), language. A vacancy whose knockouts you fail is not packaged, whatever the fit.
D2. Hard-language-required vacancies are filtered at screening, not "risked". Preferred-language stays in play with honest disclosure of your level.
D3. Each package README suggests one human contact route when findable (named recruiter on the posting, team lead on LinkedIn) with a 3-line draft note you can send yourself. Referral routes beat the queue several-fold.
D4. Note expected assessments per employer where known (skills test, video interview, games at big graduate schemes) so you are not surprised.
D5. Top-third check: cover the recruiter's 6 to 40 second skim. If the fit case is not already made in the top third of page one, rewrite.

## E. The audit checklist (run per package, both blocking auditors)

- [ ] Single column, no graphics, contact at top of body, one page
- [ ] Every claim traced to EVIDENCE-INDEX.md; never-claim list respected
- [ ] Vacancy's hard skills mirrored exactly where honestly true
- [ ] Noisy two-letter tokens spelled out; clean acronyms doubled once
- [ ] All bullets action + method + verified number
- [ ] Profile and title tailored to this vacancy; company-name test passed on the letter
- [ ] Knockouts answerable; language requirement respected
- [ ] No em dashes, no recycled sentences, no stuffing, no fluff
- [ ] DOCX and PDF both generated, opened, and visually checked
- [ ] Values verdict recorded with reasoning in the README (if the values module is on)
