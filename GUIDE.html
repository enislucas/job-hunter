Job Hunter · the friendly guide

▲ Job Hunter, the lazy way

This is the whole thing in plain words. No jargon. If you can double-click an icon and type one word, you can run this. Boxes are remembered on this computer.

The one promise: Job Hunter never sends anything. It prepares applications and lays them out for you; you press every send button yourself. It all runs on your own machine and nothing leaves it.

Part 1 · Get it running (about 20 minutes, once)

1. Put the folder on your computer Clone or download this Job Hunter folder somewhere you like. Open it in Claude Code (or your coding agent) so that folder is the working directory.
You should see: the folder with CLAUDE.md, dashboard.html, a runner folder, and this guide inside it.
2. Start the little helper (the "saver") Open SETUP.md and follow the saver steps: run it once, then make it start automatically, then make a desktop icon. Copy-paste the ready-made commands; they figure out the paths for you.
You should see: a Job Hunter icon on your desktop, and later a small green dot in the app meaning the helper is awake.
If the green dot is red: no problem. The app still works; it just saves your feedback by downloading a file, which the next run picks up from your Downloads folder automatically.
3. Tell it who you are (once) Either fill in SETUP.md by hand, or just do the next step and let it interview you. It needs your real skills and projects so your CV stays honest, your fields and city so it hunts the right jobs, and (optional) any ethical line you want it to respect.
You should see: the demo data replaced by your own once you finish. Until then, everything on screen is clearly marked "demo".

Part 2 · Use it (every time)

4. Say the magic word: Go In the Job Hunter Claude session, type Go. On the very first run it interviews you and sets everything up. After that, every Go hunts fresh jobs and prepares a few complete applications for you.
You should see: the run work through hunting, filtering, and packaging, then tell you it finished and how many it prepared.
5. Open the app and read your day Double-click the desktop Job Hunter icon. The Overview shows your numbers. Applications shows each prepared job as a card, with a countdown and a rough chance. Click a card to open its page: what the job actually is, why you fit, the exact link to apply, and the two files to upload.
You should see: a dark window with a sidebar. Every card is something you can act on. The numbers are honest, and chances say "rough estimate".
6. Decide, and tell the machine On any application page: do the 3-minute quick-verify (click the link, glance at the PDF, check a number). If you like it, apply yourself with the two files, then set the status to submitted and press Commit feedback. If you do not like it, say why and press Commit. Both teach it.
You should see: a small "Committed" toast. Next run, the machine remembers your choice and gets a little sharper.
If you disagree with anything (a chance, a filter, a values verdict): just say so in the feedback box or in inputs\corrections.md. Your word always wins.

Part 3 · The two things worth knowing

7. The three words of the Forge Once a month, Job Hunter suggests one portfolio project to build, shaped by what employers are actually asking for. If you accept, it becomes a guided build where YOU write the code and it tutors you. You steer with one word at a time: hint (a nudge, you write the code), help (it writes the smallest piece and explains it), nuclear (it builds the rest, honestly labelled). Everything your own hands write is tracked, so your CV stays truthful about what you built.
You should see: your skills genuinely improving, and an honest story for interviews about what you made versus what the AI made.
8. What never happens It never submits an application, never sends an email, never posts, never makes an account, never spends money. Every claim on your CV must trace to real evidence or it does not ship, so you can walk into any interview relaxed. Anything doubtful waits for you, never guesses.
You should see: nothing leave your computer without you doing it. That is the whole design.
