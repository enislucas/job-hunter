## Grounding (halal example; replace with your own basis if using a different floor)

- Riba (interest) is condemned in the Quran with great severity: al-Baqarah 2:275-279.
- The Prophet, salla Allahu alayhi wa sallam, cursed the consumer of riba, its payer, its scribe, and its two witnesses, and said they are all alike (Sahih Muslim 1598). The scribe and witnesses earn nothing from the riba itself; they are cursed for serving the operation. Principle: proximity of service to a forbidden core operation is itself the problem, even when the individual desk task looks innocent.
- General principle of not assisting in sin: al-Ma'idah 5:2, "do not cooperate in sin and transgression."
- Consequence for employment, per mainstream contemporary fatwa for interest-core institutions: no role at all inside a business whose core is riba, however clean the individual desk. Screening thresholds used for stock investing (tolerated minor revenue percentages) are NOT imported here for interest-core employers; employment is service, not shareholding.

## The three verdicts

- CLEAR: core business permitted, and the role does not directly serve a forbidden revenue stream.
- EXCLUDED: forbidden-core business, any role; or a permitted-core business where THIS role directly serves the forbidden segment.
- ASK: mixed or unclear revenue, scholarly difference, or role-proximity doubt. Never packaged, never auto-cleared. Goes to the brief's values queue as a fully formed question for whoever you escalate to.

## Cached rulings (REPLACE with your own; the format only)

Store authoritative answers in memory\shaykh-rulings.md and summarize the binding ones here. The example below is FICTIONAL, present only to show the entry shape:

```
SR-001 (FICTIONAL EXAMPLE), aggregate-logistics case:
Planning logistics where some shipments may contain an excluded product is permitted when the
planner cannot know and cannot check which shipments contain it. Generalizes only to roles with
the same structure: aggregate planning, no item-level knowledge, no ability to inquire.

SR-002 (FICTIONAL EXAMPLE), item-level-visibility case:
When the person in the role KNOWS which orders contain the excluded product (age checks,
item-level visibility), the work is impermissible.
```

The knowledge hinge (drawn from the two example rulings above): knowledge is the pivot. When the excluded goods are identifiable to the person in the role (item-level visibility, named excluded lines in the data), the work is impermissible; when the flow is genuinely anonymized and unknowable, the aggregate-work permission applies.

## Verdict procedure (per company, cache in memory\companies.md)

1. Core business: what actually earns the money? Company site, annual report summary, encyclopedia, business registries. Ten minutes of honest reading, not vibes.
2. Category match against the table. Forbidden-core: EXCLUDED, stop.
3. Role proximity: does this specific role serve a forbidden stream (a data role optimizing an excluded product line inside an otherwise CLEAR retailer is EXCLUDED; the same retailer's neutral logistics analytics is CLEAR).
4. Doubt remaining: ASK, write the question, done. Never resolve doubt by optimism.
5. Record verdict, reasoning, date, sources in memory\companies.md. Verdicts expire after 12 months.

## Escalation question template (what lands in the brief's values queue)

```
Company: <name>. What they do: <two honest sentences with the revenue reality and sources>.
The role: <title, what the work actually is>.
The doubt: <the specific question, for example: the firm's clients include interest-core banks;
this role would sit in non-bank projects; is employment there permitted?>
```

Written so it can be read to whoever you escalate to in thirty seconds. One question per company, no bundling.

## Practice feasibility (noted in every package README, never a verdict)

For a halal filter these are prayer and community fit; adapt to whatever practical constraints matter to you.

- Five daily prayers: any workplace signal on flexibility, quiet rooms, break culture.
- Jumu'ah: Friday midday absence of 60 to 90 minutes; office distance to a masjid.
- Social culture: alcohol-centered team events noted honestly; attendance is the user's choice, not a verdict matter.
- Personal-conduct norms: nothing to research per employer; the user's own conduct covers it.

Unknowns are written as unknowns. No guessing in either direction.
